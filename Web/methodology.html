Working Paper · Methodology

How This Network Was Built

Data sources, construction pipeline, and the four analytical methods behind the figures — betweenness centrality, HHI concentration, percolation, and multiplex participation.

1 · Data Sources

The base network models global semiconductor trade as a six-layer directed graph of 24 countries and 23 firms (47 nodes, 513 edges). Country-level flows are biproportional inferences from aggregate trade statistics; firm-level flows and financial figures are drawn from public disclosures. This is a working paper — edge weights are estimates, not audited figures.

2 · Construction Pipeline

  1. Raw trade and financial data cleaned and merged in R (tidyverse, readxl, igraph).
  2. Country-level bilateral flows disaggregated to the six supply-chain layers using HS-code mappings.
  3. Firm nodes added where a single corporation plausibly accounts for a large share of a layer's global capacity (e.g. ASML in EUV lithography), with edges to/from their home country and known customer/supplier countries.
  4. Betweenness centrality computed per layer and on the aggregate graph (Brandes' algorithm).
  5. Downstream metrics — HHI, percolation, multiplex participation — computed directly from the resulting node/edge set (see §4–6).

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index is the sum of squared market shares within a market:

Here, si is a node's share of total source-side (export) edge weight within a single layer. HHI ranges from near-0 (many equal-sized competitors) to 1.0 (single supplier). Conventionally, HHI > 0.25 marks high concentration.

Computed directly from the edge set: Raw Extraction is the standout at HHI = 0.553 (China, 72% of source-side flow — chiefly primary gallium). Every other layer sits below the 0.25 threshold on this pure export-share measure — Refined Materials 0.189, Design & IP 0.157, Fabrication 0.125, Assembly & Packaging 0.144, Equipment lowest at 0.114.

This is a country/firm export-share HHI, not a firm-ownership HHI. Equipment and Design & IP look dispersed here because the measure is computed at the node level and several distinct US-headquartered firms (Applied Materials, Lam Research, KLA, Cadence, Synopsys) are counted separately even though they share a national jurisdiction and, in practice, considerable strategic alignment. A firm-only HHI restricted to those layers would read meaningfully higher.

5 · Percolation — Targeted Attack vs. Random Failure

To test structural fragility directly, we simulate two failure modes on the aggregate (all-layers, undirected, simple-graph) network and track the size of the giant connected component as nodes are removed:

  • Targeted attack, recalculated: at each step, remove the node with the currently-highest betweenness centrality, then recompute betweenness on the reduced graph before the next removal. This is the harder, more realistic attack scenario — it accounts for centrality shifting onto new nodes as the network degrades.
  • Random failure: nodes removed in random order, averaged over 200 trials.

Result: the giant component drops below 50% of its original size after removing only 17.0% of nodes under targeted attack, versus 46.8% under random failure — a robust-yet-fragile signature consistent with scale-free network topology, and the same qualitative pattern found in this author's prior AviationNet analysis of the global commercial flight network.

where L = 6 layers and is node i's betweenness in layer α. Overall Multiplex Centrality (OMC) sums a node's betweenness across all layers; the Participation Coefficient measures how evenly that centrality is spread — 0 for a node powerful in exactly one layer, approaching 1 for a node with equal centrality spread across all six.

Almost every marquee firm in this dataset — ASML, TSMC, Arm, Cadence/Synopsys — scores a participation coefficient of exactly 0: enormous depth, confined to a single layer. China is the sole outlier with genuine cross-layer breadth (participation ≈ 0.77, active in four of six layers), making it the only actor in the network with both scale and structural reach at once.

7 · Companion Figure — Supplier-Spend Sankey

The Sankey diagram on the main page is a separate data lineage from the trade-statistics network above. Its framing and sourcing approach is credited to Micro on YouTube, who compiled a supplier-spend-weighted diagram from investor-relations disclosures and SEC/6-K filings, inferring undisclosed relationships by working backwards from listed suppliers among the public companies. This site's version follows the same method independently against a subset of those primary sources, with each flow tagged as either directly disclosed in a filing or an order-of-magnitude inference from documented (but not dollar-quantified) relationships. 8 · Theoretical Framework

Infrastructural power (Braun). Power that derives from control over critical infrastructure rather than ownership of assets or formal diplomatic authority. TSMC's and ASML's leverage is infrastructural — it operates through network position, not treaty.

Actor-network theory (Latour). Refuses the human/non-human distinction. ASML's EUV machines and Japan's photoresist chemistry are enrolled as actants, not neutral substrates — chokepoints are effects of stabilised actor-networks, not pre-given structural features.

Network formation games (Jackson & Wolinsky). Predicts that when direct link costs exceed the marginal benefit of bypassing a hub, the equilibrium topology is a star. This is the formal reason decoupling from TSMC is not individually rational under current cost structures.

9 · Limitations

  • Edge weights are biproportional inferences from country-level aggregates, not audited firm-to-firm transaction data.
  • The Sankey figure mixes disclosed figures with order-of-magnitude estimates, explicitly flagged per flow.
  • HHI is computed on source-side export share, not firm ownership or revenue concentration — see the disclaimer in §4.
  • Percolation analysis uses a simple, undirected collapse of the six-layer graph; layer-specific robustness may differ from the aggregate picture shown.
  • This is a working paper. Figures will be revised as the underlying pipeline is updated.
